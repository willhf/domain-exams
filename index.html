What Domain Exams are

Domain Exams are independent, written examinations designed to assess mastery of undergraduate-level subjects, especially in mathematics and theoretical computer science. Exams emphasize correct reasoning and proof-based justification where appropriate, with clearly defined scope and standards.

  • Written and proof-based — not multiple choice.
  • Standards, not ranking — results reflect absolute performance, not relative to others.
  • Depth over breadth — narrow in scope.
  • Reasoning-first — emphasizes understanding over memorization.

Initial subjects may include linear algebra, probability, discrete mathematics, and algorithms.

Detailed exam scopes and standards will be published prior to launch.

What you receive

  • A result reported as one of: Mastery, Proficiency, Partial, or Insufficient (standards-based, not rank-ordered).
  • A printed certificate for outcomes of Mastery or Proficiency.
  • A shareable result page under an opaque, unguessable URL (not indexed for discovery).
