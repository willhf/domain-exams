What Domain Exams Are

Domain Exams are independent, written examinations that assess mastery of undergraduate-level material in mathematics and theoretical computer science. They emphasize correct reasoning and proof-based justification, with clearly defined scope and standards.

  • Written and proof-based — not multiple choice.
  • Standards, not ranking — results reflect absolute performance.
  • Depth over breadth — each exam is narrow but rigorous.
  • Reasoning-first — understanding over memorization.

Initial subjects are planned to include:

  • Algorithms and Data Structures
  • Linear Algebra
  • Probability
  • Discrete Mathematics

Detailed exam scopes and standards will be published prior to launch.

Reasons to Participate

  • Show that your understanding meets honors-level undergraduate standards.
  • Give your self-study a clear external objective and endpoint.
  • Obtain a durable, independent record of subject mastery.

What You Receive

  • A result of Mastery, Proficiency, Partial, or Insufficient.
  • A printed certificate for outcomes of Mastery or Proficiency.
  • A shareable result page under an opaque, unguessable URL (not indexed).
