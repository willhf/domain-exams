The Vision

Textbooks are cheap, online courses are everywhere, and AI tutors are widely available. Access to learning is no longer the bottleneck.

What's missing is validation.

Domain Exams fixes that.

We envision a world where anyone with the discipline to master a subject independently can earn a durable, credible signal of that achievement.

What Domain Exams Are

Domain Exams are independent, written examinations that assess mastery of undergraduate-level material in mathematics and theoretical computer science. They emphasize correct reasoning and proof-based justification, with clearly defined scope and standards.

  • Written and proof-based — not multiple choice.
  • Standards, not ranking — results reflect absolute performance.
  • Depth over breadth — each exam is narrow but rigorous.
  • Reasoning-first — understanding over memorization.

Subjects will include Linear Algebra, Algorithms and Data Structures, Probability, Discrete Mathematics, Theory of Computation, and others. Detailed exam scopes and standards will be published prior to launch.

Why Participate

  • Demonstrate honors-level undergraduate understanding.
  • Turn self-study into a clear, external objective.
  • Earn an independent, durable signal of mastery.

What You Receive

  • A result of Mastery, Proficiency, Partial, or Insufficient.
  • A printed certificate for outcomes of Mastery or Proficiency.
  • A shareable result page under an unguessable URL (not indexed).
